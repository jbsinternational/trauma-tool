Trauma and Violence Tools

For more information about JBS International’s expertise and project work in the area of trauma and violence, please visit www.jbsinternational.com/expertise/trauma-violence.

To prevent and reduce the negative impact of exposure to traumatic events, it is critical to acknowledge trauma, identify signs and symptoms, and support individuals, families, agencies and communities that are exposed to traumatic events. A trauma-informed care approach to supporting all age groups, organizations and communities is moving away from the question “What is wrong with you?” to “What happened to you?” JBS is supporting and helping to enhance trauma informed practices through an array of government partnerships with award-winning communication and outreach efforts – resource tools, publications, e-learning tools, and social media campaigns – that address individuals of all ages who have experienced trauma. All of these projects are directed in helping anybody working with individuals who have experienced trauma, academia, and communities accelerate their work on prevention and treatment to help individuals and societies heal from trauma and build resilience.

Trauma Informed Care: Perspectives and Resources

Many resources, actions, and lessons learned from entities that have become trauma informed, are necessary to help all child-serving systems and provider organizations become trauma-informed. Funded through the the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) PRTF Demonstration Program, the National Technical Assistance Center for Children’s Mental Health at Georgetown University (TA Center) and JBS International (JBS) created this comprehensive, web-based and video-enhanced resource tool to support leaders, decision-makers, and providers in all child-serving systems and at all levels (national, state, tribal, territorial and local) on that journey. This resource tool provides information everyone needs to know about the impact of childhood trauma and how to address it.
